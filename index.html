Chapter 1

The Beginning of Horan

I was not born under this sky. I do not belong to this fragile world of dust and breath.

I come from ESKAS, a civilization that does not merely live in time but bends it, reshapes it, commands it. We are what lesser minds would call a Type V civilization—architects of reality itself. Stars are our tools. Time is our language.

And yet, even gods can starve.

At the heart of ESKAS lies Exlir, the pulse of our existence—the mineral that fuels our dominion over time and matter. It is ancient, unforgiving, and rare; a resource that takes millions of years to form. Once abundant, now fading. And without it, we are nothing.

So we began the hunt.

World after world, galaxy after galaxy, we searched. We calculated. We conquered. And when a planet failed to yield even a trace of Exlir, we erased it. Not out of cruelty, but necessity. That is what they taught us. That is what I believed.

Until the day I failed.

I was sent to a world teeming with life, a world that laughed, dreamed, struggled just like this one. I walked among them unseen, studying their skies, their soil, their souls. And for the first time, I hesitated. They were not resources. They were alive.

I reported nothing. I delayed the inevitable. But ESKAS does not tolerate hesitation. They came. And they burned the world to silence.

That day, something inside me fractured—something no power could repair. Now I am no longer a seeker. I am an executioner. Sent from planet to planet carrying a single command: find Exlir or destroy everything.

And then I found this place.

At first, it seemed ordinary. The gravity, the elements, the life—all within expected parameters. But something... something beneath the surface called to me. Not Exlir, not exactly. But close. A resonance. A frequency that echoes what we lost.

For the first time since my exile, I felt hope. But this world is different. It hides its secrets well. The deeper I search, the more it resists. There are patterns here I cannot predict, forces I cannot control, truths that refuse to be rewritten.

And the more I uncover, the more I question: Am I here to save my civilization or to destroy another innocent world?

Because if this planet holds the key to Exlir, then ESKAS will come. And when they do, time itself will not be enough to save it.
